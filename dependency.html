What is Online Game Addiction?

Online game addiction, also known as gaming addiction or internet gaming disorder, refers to a compulsive and excessive use of online video games that leads to negative consequences in various areas of a person's life. It is characterized by an individual's inability to control their gaming habits, resulting in a significant impairment in their daily functioning, social interactions, and overall well-being.

Signs and symptoms of online game addiction

Preoccupation with gaming:

Constantly thinking about gaming, even when not playing, and feeling a strong urge to play

Loss of interest in other activities:

Neglecting or losing interest in previously enjoyed hobbies, socializing, or academic/work-related activities in favor of gaming.

Loss of control:

Being unable to limit or stop gaming despite attempts to do so, spending excessive time playing games, and neglecting other important aspects of life.

Physical health issues:

Neglecting physical well-being, such as lack of sleep, poor diet, and sedentary lifestyle, which can lead to health problems like obesity, backaches, and carpal tunnel syndrome.

Withdrawal symptoms:

Irritability, restlessness, moodiness, or anxiety when unable to play games or when attempting to cut down or stop gaming.

Online games Addiction

It's important to note that not everyone who plays online games excessively is addicted. The diagnosis of gaming disorder involves a persistent pattern of problematic gaming behavior that significantly impairs various areas of life and lasts for at least 12 months, as outlined by the World Health Organization's International Classification of Diseases (ICD-11) criteria. If someone suspects they or a loved one may be struggling with online game addiction, seeking professional help from mental health experts, such as psychologists or addiction counselors, can provide guidance and support in managing and overcoming the addiction.
